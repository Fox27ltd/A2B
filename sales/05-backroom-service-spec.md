# Backroom — the £150/month upsell

*Do not pitch this tomorrow. This is the email you send 3-4 weeks after they're live, when the site has settled in and they trust you.*

---

## What you're selling

A weekly business brief delivered to the owner's WhatsApp or email, every Monday morning at 8am. Plus optional invoice chasing.

What the owner gets each Monday is a 30-second read that tells them: *did last week go well, what's coming this week, who hasn't paid, anything to worry about.* No login, no dashboard, no app to learn.

What you're really selling is **"someone is paying attention to my business so I don't have to."** That's worth a lot to a working garage owner.

That's it. Reply "yes both" or "yes Patel, leave Smith" — Cowork sends the chase email (with your approval, on the owner's behalf), and drops a "follow up Fri" event in their Google Calendar.

## What it actually takes you to deliver

**Setup — one time, ~2 hours total per client:**

1. Connect their QuickBooks (or Xero) to your Cowork workspace
2. Connect their Gmail (read-only on the relevant inbox)
3. Connect Google Calendar (to drop chase reminders)
4. Build the message template in their Cowork project
5. Test the first Monday brief with them on the phone — refine wording, confirm what they care about most

**Ongoing — 15-20 minutes per Monday:**

1. Run `business-pulse` skill against their connected stack
2. Skim the output — sanity check, adjust tone
3. Send to WhatsApp / email
4. If they reply with chase approvals, run `invoice-chase` skill, ack to owner

That's it. 4-5 hours of your time per client per month. £150 - £35 (rough Cowork cost share) - £0 (no extra hosting) = **~£115/month margin**. At 10 clients that's £1,150/month recurring on top of your £550 storefront monthly.

## When to pitch it

3-4 weeks after launch. You're already in their inbox monthly for the £55 storefront bill. Add a line:

> While I've got your QuickBooks on screen — fancy me running a Monday brief on the business? Cash position, this week's bookings, who hasn't paid. Straight to WhatsApp. £150 a month, you do nothing. I'll set it up next week, first one's free, you tell me if it's useful.

Most will say yes to a free trial. Most who try it will keep it. The ones who don't keep it cost you 2 hours and you learn what to refine for the next garage.

---

## What you DON'T do

- You don't give them tax advice. ("This is what your books say, talk to your accountant about decisions.")
- You don't act as their accountant or bookkeeper. ("I report numbers, your accountant interprets them.")
- You don't make decisions for them. ("Want me to chase these? Want me to leave them?" — always their choice.)
- You don't store their data. Cowork runs on your machine against their connected accounts. You don't keep CSVs of their financials on your hard drive.

Staying on the right side of these keeps you well clear of AML registration, FCA territory, and bookkeeper-grade professional indemnity insurance. **Report-only is unregulated. Advice-or-decision flips that.**

## Upgrade — Backroom Full at £325/month

The £150 version is "Pulse". Once they're hooked, the upsell:

- Weekly Pulse brief (same as £150)
- + monthly 30-min Zoom call to walk through the month
- + you handle ALL invoice chasing (they don't even have to approve, you send the chases on a 30/45/60-day cadence and copy them in)
- + quarterly margin review (1-page PDF: which services are profitable, which aren't, year-on-year)

This is closer to a fractional FD. Worth £325 in the SMB market because their alternative is hiring a bookkeeper at £600+/month.

## Tracking your time

Build a spreadsheet column for each Backroom client called "Mon mins". If a client routinely takes 40+ minutes, either the brief is too detailed (cut it back) or they need the Full tier (upsell). Don't subsidise the £150 tier with hours that should be £325.
