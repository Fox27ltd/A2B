# Tomorrow — A2B pitch prep checklist

*Print this. Tick it off the morning of.*

---

## The night before

- [ ] Phase 9 deploy is done and the prod URL works on a phone (test from your own phone on 4G, not just wifi)
- [ ] Open the URL on your laptop browser — load homepage, scroll the hero, check the scrub is smooth
- [ ] Click into every nav page — confirm none 404
- [ ] Try the quote form — confirm it opens your mail app pre-filled
- [ ] Try "Get directions" on the map — confirm it opens Google Maps with A2B as destination
- [ ] Print **two** copies of `02-pricing-onepager.md` (front and back, A4)
- [ ] Print **two** copies of `03-web-design-agreement.md` (you keep one, they keep one if they sign)
- [ ] Charge laptop. Charge phone. Bring a charger.
- [ ] Read `01-pitch-script.md` once before bed

## The morning of

- [ ] Open laptop. Pull the site up. Leave the browser tab open.
- [ ] Check the dev server isn't still running on localhost (close that terminal — show prod, not dev)
- [ ] Phone fully charged
- [ ] Wear something credible-but-not-corporate — clean shirt, no suit. Trade-friendly.
- [ ] Bring two pens (you'll forget where one goes)
- [ ] Bring the printouts in a plain envelope or folder. Not a clipboard.

## What to take in

- Laptop with site loaded
- Phone (for showing the mobile version, and for taking their number)
- 2x pricing one-pager
- 2x agreement
- Pen × 2
- Business card if you have one — if not, a number scrawled on the pricing sheet is fine

## What NOT to take in

- A pitch deck
- A clipboard
- A briefcase
- Anything that looks like you sell vacuum cleaners door to door

## Timing

- Aim for **mid-morning Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday** — Mondays are catch-up chaos, Fridays are clear-the-deck busy. Mid-morning mid-week is when garage owners are caught up and bored at the counter.
- Avoid lunch hour (12-2) and the end-of-day rush (4-5:30).
- 11am Tuesday is the sweet spot.

## The ask

You're aiming to leave with **one** of:
1. **A signed agreement** — best outcome, fold it in the envelope, walk out, send the deposit invoice within 2 hours
2. **Their phone number + a sent text with the URL** — second best, "I'll follow up Wednesday morning"
3. **A polite no** — fine. Don't push. Move on. Plenty of garages.

What you DON'T leave with:
- "I'll think about it" without a phone number → polite no
- "Send me an email" without a phone number → also polite no
- A long browse with nothing committed → you talked too much, let the site do the work next time

## After the meeting

- [ ] Within 30 minutes: text the link with a one-liner ("Cheers for the time — here's the preview, anything to ask just call. Cal")
- [ ] Within 24 hours: if signed, send onboarding email (template `04-onboarding-email.md`)
- [ ] Within 48 hours: if not signed, one follow-up text. Then leave it.
- [ ] Either way: write a 2-line note in your sales spreadsheet — what worked, what didn't, what to do differently next garage

## If they say yes on the spot

- [ ] Sign your half of the agreement, hand them their copy signed
- [ ] Take a phone photo of their signed copy as backup
- [ ] Take 50% deposit by bank transfer there and then (have your bank details ready on your phone)
- [ ] OR send invoice within 2 hours if cash isn't possible at the counter
- [ ] Within 24 hours: send onboarding email + start work
