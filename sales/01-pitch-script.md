# Pitch script — A2B Autocentre

**Setting:** counter at the garage, owner / family member is free for 5 mins, you've got the laptop open and the site loaded at the prod URL (or `localhost:3000`).

**Goal of the meeting:** get permission to send them the link so they can show the family, then get a "yes, let's do it" within 48 hours.

---

## The opener (30 seconds)

> "Morning — got two minutes? My name's Callum, I run a small studio called Fox27. I build websites for independent garages. I spent the weekend mocking up what your site could look like — same content, but built modern. I wanted you to see it first before I bin it. Mind if I show you on the laptop?"

**Why this works:** no pressure, no pitch deck, no "have you ever considered…" — just *I've done the work, want to look?* People say yes to that.

---

## The walkthrough (2 minutes — let the site do the work)

Open at the homepage. Don't talk for the first 5 seconds — let them see the hero load. Then narrate, slowly:

**1. Hero scrub.** *Scroll down through it.*
> "This is the bit I'm proud of. You scroll and the warning lights go out, right to left across the dashboard. The idea is — you sort cars, you make problems disappear. The whole site opens with that idea."

**2. Trust strip + Quote form.** *Scroll past, land on the form.*
> "Underneath: RAC Approved, family-run since 2016, Rainham. Then a quote form — straight to your inbox. Customer types in their reg, what's wrong, hits send, you get an email two seconds later."

**3. Find Us.** *Scroll to the map.*
> "Map's pinned on your actual postcode. Customer can hit 'Get Directions' and it opens Google Maps on their phone with you as the destination. No 'how do I find you' phone calls."

**4. Sub-pages.** *Click into Servicing, then MOT.*
> "Full page on servicing — Bronze, Silver, Gold. MOT page from £35. Repairs page covers everything you do — diagnostics, gearbox, cambelt, all of it. Every page has the quote button."

**5. The nav.** *Click around quickly.*
> "Works on mobile too — most people will see it on their phone."

Total: 2 minutes. Stop talking. Let them react.

---

## What they'll probably say + how you respond

**"Looks good — what's it cost?"**
> "Two options. Either £1,500 once, you own everything, the keys are yours. Or £750 to build, £55 a month and I look after it forever — backups, updates, copy changes, the lot. Most garages pick monthly because it's never a phone call you have to make again. Either way you're live within a week."

**"We've already got a website."**
> "I saw — Fair Technologies built it, about 2018 by the look. About page still has placeholder tags in it. Customers don't care that you've got one, they care whether it makes them call you. I'd bet you yours doesn't. This one would."

**"Send me the link, I'll have a think."**
> "Course. What's your number? I'll text it now. I'll follow up Wednesday — if you say no, no hard feelings. If you say yes I can have you live by Friday."
> [Take their number. Text the link as you stand there.]

**"What about my logo / photos?"**
> "Drop me your logo by email and I'll have it in by tomorrow. If you've got any workshop photos that's a bonus — if not, the ones on your Google Business Profile are good enough to start."

**"Who'll see this site?"**
> "Anyone who searches MOT Rainham, garage Rainham, car service Upminster — the whole site's built to rank for those. Plus everyone you currently hand a card to, they Google you the same evening."

**"Can it take card payments / bookings online?"**
> "Not today, on purpose — you'd be paying for something most of your customers won't use. If you want it later, easy add. For now, phone bookings work."

**"What if I want changes?"**
> "Hour changes, price changes, photo swaps — included. Bigger stuff like a new page, I'll quote you separately. Most months you won't ask for anything."

## The Backroom upsell — DO NOT pitch this tomorrow

Don't mention it. They've got enough to take in. Sell the website first. Three weeks after launch, when they're happy:

> "While I've got your monthly QuickBooks open for billing — fancy me running you a Monday brief? Cash position, this week's bookings, who hasn't paid. Straight to WhatsApp. £150 a month, you do nothing."

That's the £150/month upsell. See `05-backroom-service-spec.md`.

---

## The close

Aim to leave with **one of**:
- A signed Storefront agreement (best — see `03-web-design-agreement.md`)
- Their phone number + a fired-off text with the live URL + a confirmed time to follow up
- A "no thanks" — fine, move on to the next garage

**Don't leave with:** "I'll think about it, get back to you." That's a polite no. If they're not ready to give you their number, they're not buying.

---

## Vibe notes

- They're trade. They smell BS instantly. Be direct, undersell rather than over-promise.
- Don't slag off the current site too hard — Fair Technologies might be a mate.
- £1,500 is real money to a garage owner. Frame the monthly model as "this hour's worth of MOTs covers the whole year."
- If they're not the decision-maker (employee on the counter), get the owner's number and come back.
- Wear something that says "I work for myself" not "I'm a corporate web sales guy." Garages trust scruffy + competent.
