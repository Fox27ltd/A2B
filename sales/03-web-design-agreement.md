## 1. What we're doing

The Studio will design, build, and deliver a website for the Client at the agreed domain. The site will include the pages, content, and functionality described in **Schedule A** (overleaf).

## 2. Choose your model

The Client chooses **one** of the following:

- [ ] **One-off purchase — £1,500 + VAT.** Site is built, delivered, and the Client takes full ownership of the code, domain, and hosting account. Studio's obligations end at handover.
- [ ] **Build + Monthly — £750 + VAT setup, £55 + VAT/month.** Studio retains hosting, monitoring, backups, and includes routine content updates as described in clause 5. Code ownership transfers to the Client after 12 consecutive months of monthly payments, or at any time if the Client pays the £1,500 buy-out.

## 4. Payment terms

- **One-off model:** 50% (£750 + VAT) on signing. 50% on go-live.
- **Monthly model:** £750 + VAT on signing. £55 + VAT/month by direct debit, starting 30 days after go-live. First month free.
- Late payment interest is charged at 8% above the Bank of England base rate from the due date, in line with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

## 5. What "monthly" includes (monthly model only)

Each month, while the Client is paid up:

- Hosting, SSL certificate, domain renewal
- 24/7 uptime monitoring; the Studio will respond to any outage within 4 working hours
- Daily off-site backups, 30-day retention
- Security patches to underlying frameworks
- Up to **30 minutes** of copy changes (opening hours, prices, photos, phone numbers, new reviews) per calendar month. Unused minutes don't roll over.
- Email support; response within 1 working day

**Out of scope** — chargeable at £80 + VAT per hour, quoted up front:
- New pages or new sections
- New features (online booking, e-commerce, payments)
- Photography
- Copywriting beyond basic edits
- Logo design or rebrand
- Custom integrations (CRM, calendar sync, analytics setup)

## 6. Cancellation

- Monthly clients may cancel with 30 days' written notice (email is fine).
- On cancellation: the Client may take a full export of their content and the live site files, free of charge. The domain is theirs.
- If cancelled within the first 12 months, code ownership has not transferred — the Client may purchase outright for the difference between £1,500 and what they've paid in setup + monthly fees, prorated.
- Studio may terminate immediately if invoices are 30+ days overdue.

## 7. Intellectual property

- Content the Client supplies (logo, photos, copy, reviews, business name) remains the Client's property.
- Code written by the Studio is licensed to the Client for the duration of the agreement under the monthly model, and transferred outright under the one-off model (or after 12 months on monthly).
- The Studio retains the right to reference the work in its own portfolio and case studies. The Client may opt out by notifying the Studio in writing.
- "RAC Approved" and any RAC-related imagery are trademarks of RAC Motoring Services and are used by the Client under its own licence with the RAC.

## 8. Confidentiality

Each party will treat the other's commercial information as confidential and won't disclose it to third parties without consent, except where required by law.

## 9. Data protection

The Studio acts as a data processor when handling form submissions from the Client's website. Both parties will comply with UK GDPR. Personal data passes from the visitor's browser to the Client's email inbox — the Studio does not store or read submissions, unless a separate hosted-form service (e.g. Formspree, Resend) is added by mutual agreement, in which case a separate Data Processing Agreement will be put in place.

## 10. Liability

- Neither party excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or any liability which cannot lawfully be excluded.
- Subject to that, the Studio's maximum liability under this agreement is capped at the total fees paid by the Client in the 12 months preceding the claim.
- Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential losses (lost profits, lost data not held by either party, business interruption).

## 11. Force majeure

Neither party is in breach if performance is prevented by events beyond reasonable control (including but not limited to: third-party hosting outages, internet failures, government action, natural disasters).

## 12. Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Any dispute will be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

This document, together with Schedules A and B, is the complete agreement between the parties. It replaces any prior discussion or proposal. Any change must be in writing and signed by both parties.
